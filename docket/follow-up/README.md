# Follow-up

A next step that **has to wait** — on time, on real data, on precious human cycles, or on an
external event. The deferral is what *makes* it a follow-up: if it could just be done now it'd be
done inline; if skipping it would leave something broken it's a must-do, not this. It may or may not
come due — and when it does, it costs real attention.

## What to capture

A pointer plus a headline, not a spec — the next step and *why it waits* (the time, the data, the
cycles, the event). The session-provenance footer carries how it came up; the entry just needs the
step and its trigger.

```markdown
---
area: document-search
title: set the reranker min_score cutoff from real query data
---

**Next step.** Pick the `min_score` threshold from the live score distribution — too high drops
good hits, too low lets noise through.

**Waits on.** A week of real queries, so the call is data-driven rather than guessed.
```

## Ask before filing

- Why does it have to wait — time, data, precious human cycles, or an external event?
- Could it just be done now (→ inline), or is it broken if skipped (→ a must-do, not a follow-up)?
- What's the concrete next step, and what triggers it?
