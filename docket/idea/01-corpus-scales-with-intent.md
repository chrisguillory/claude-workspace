title: corpus breadth scales with intent abstraction
---

**The spark.** Where a docket entry's *truth* lives slides by type — and it's an axis, not an
accident: the more concrete and local the intent, the narrower its ground-truth corpus; the more
generative and abstract, the broader.

`tech-debt` (the codebase only) → `follow-up` (the one session that surfaced it) → `feature` (code
+ the "why") → `idea` (code + *all* sessions + the open web).

Bug-fixing needs the file; ideation needs the world.

**Why it's exciting.** It *dictates the tooling per type* — you mine `tech-debt` from the code; you
mine and *enrich* `idea`s from the session corpus (semantic neighbours, prior discussion) and, at
the far end, the web. "Where do I look?" stops being ad-hoc and becomes a property of the type. The
corollary that sharpens it: a debt that lived only in a session and was never codified was either a
gap that should've been closed then, or deliberate and moot — which is *why* `tech-debt`'s corpus is
rightly code-only. This is the spine the rest of the vision hangs on (the session-miner, code-miner,
daydream, corollary-enrichment all read whatever corpus the type implies).

**Shape & edge cases.** The corpus is used two ways per type: *where you mine to find* matters of
that type, and *where you look to align* one you're filing. `feature` is the fuzzy point — placed
code-anchored with a little session "why," but a net-new capability may pull more world context than
that.

**Open questions.** Four discrete points or a true continuum? Where exactly does `feature` sit? And
is the web in scope at *capture* time for an `idea`, or only once it graduates into work?
